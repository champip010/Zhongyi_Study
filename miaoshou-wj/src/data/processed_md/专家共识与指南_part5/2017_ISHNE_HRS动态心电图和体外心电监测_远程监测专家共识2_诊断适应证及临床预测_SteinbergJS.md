AbstractAmbulatory ECG(AECG） is very commonly employed in a variety of clinical contexts to detect cardiac arhythmias and arhythmia paterns which are not readily obtained from the routine ECG.Prodigious amounts of rhythm information derived from AECG recording system often provide reliable diagnostic references in clinic.This paper introduces the indications of ambulatory monitoring technique in the diagnosis and management of cardiovascular diseasesand evaluates its predictive value on risk stratification against specific clinical context.

Key wordsambulatory ECG monitoring；Holter;telemetry；clinical diagnosis； indication；risk stratification; prognosis
